History of the Internet

Do you ever wonder why people built the partner-of-every-human today called the internet? I bet they were very proud and didn't even imagined that it will be as big as it is today. Born from the concept or idea of connecting people for communication during the World War 2 era and in response to the fear of a nuclear strike, it continuously grew until it became like a shadow of everyone, as a very powerful tool today. From the exciting development of the first internet called the ARPANET by the US to the many "first" events like a growing child, the interconnection to regional networks in the 1980s was a sign that modern internet has begun or also known as the pre-teen stage. Many of the generation today may not even recognize the face of the first internet. Through many changes that the internet has undergone in the puberty stage like the development of protocols, the addition of many browsers and web pages when the World Wide Web was created, increasing number of networks connected, the internet has matured. Even though the internet has undergone so many changes, there are still vast opportunities and possibilities in the internet. It may die like a human being, but I think that it will evolve, like humans, into something better, hopefully.
